EyeLabs.AI

Computer Vision in Ophthalmology

Our Motivation

Deep learning (DL) in ophthalmology has significant potential in disease detection, establishing diagnosis and predicting outcomes. However, progress has been limited as real-world datasets lack availability and variety due to proprietary and privacy concerns. This introduces difficulties when training data-hungry neural networks that rely on extensive, annotated training datasets.

In order to address this problem, we utilize deep learning techniques to generate large volumes of high-quality synthetic ophthalmic images to reproduce the statistical properties of a photorealistic data.

Our aim is to help progress innovation by providing developing AI diagnostic models with synthetic training data to hasten the transition into the real world clinical setting.

WE BELIEVE IN GREAT IDEAS

There is strong interest in Ophthalmology for computer-aided medical diagnosis systems that rely on deep learning techniques. It is difficult for medical professionals to make most medical images public without patient consent.

Publicly available datasets often lack size and expert annotations, rendering them useless for the training of data-hungry neural networks.

Therefore, the design of these systems is done exclusively by researchers that have access to private data, limiting the growth and potential of this field of research.

How We Do It

Ever since the advent of Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) in 2014, synthetic data has displayed tremendous success in democratizing data for a variety of fields. We utilize similar generative models to form a hierarchical process, training a deep neural network to learn the distribution of a smaller-sized photorealistic dataset. We are then able to sample this distribution to generate images that are not the same as any of the original data, but still preserve the photorealism and structure of the original dataset.

Data Generation

Obtain real ophthalmic images to generate a large dataset for understanding variation and distrubtion.

Data Labeling

Create learned algorithms to deploy AI into the clinic and hospital setting.

Data Enhancement

Provide fast and iterative cycles of measurement and data improvement.

Distribution

Provide synthetic data for public use.
